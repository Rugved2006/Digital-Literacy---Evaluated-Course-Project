**The Dos: Best Practices for Digital Literacy**
------------------------------------------------

1.  **Do Verify Before You Share** Check the source of news or "viral" information. Use lateral reading—open a new tab and see if credible news outlets are reporting the same thing—to avoid spreading misinformation.
    
2.  **Do Audit Your Privacy Settings** Regularly check who can see your posts and personal information. Platforms change their terms of service frequently; staying on top of these ensures you aren't sharing more than intended.
    
3.  **Do Practice "Think Before You Post"** Remember that the internet is permanent. Even deleted posts can live on via screenshots. Ask yourself: "Would I be comfortable with a future employer or my family seeing this?"
    
4.  **Do Use Strong, Unique Passwords** Enable **Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)** whenever possible. Using a password manager helps you maintain unique credentials for every platform, preventing a "domino effect" if one account is breached.
    
5.  **Do Engage with Empathy** Digital literacy includes **Digital Etiquette (Netiquette)**. Remember that there is a human on the other side of the screen. Constructive disagreement is healthy; vitriol is not.
    

**The Don'ts: Common Digital Pitfalls**
---------------------------------------

1.  **Don’t Click on Suspicious Links** Be wary of "clickbait" or direct messages (DMs) from strangers—and even friends—that look out of character. These are often phishing attempts designed to steal your login credentials.
    
2.  **Don’t Overshare Personal Details** Avoid posting your real-time location, home address, phone number, or photos of your ID/boarding passes. This information is gold for identity thieves and "doxxers."
    
3.  **Don’t Fall for the "Echo Chamber"** Don't only follow people who agree with you. Digital literacy involves recognizing how algorithms work to show you content that reinforces your existing biases. Seek out diverse perspectives.
    
4.  **Don’t Feed the Trolls** Avoid engaging with accounts that exist solely to provoke or harass. Directing your energy toward bad-faith actors usually results in unnecessary stress and gives their content more visibility.
    
5.  **Don’t Neglect Your Mental Health** Don't feel obligated to be "on" 24/7. Recognize the signs of digital fatigue and social comparison. Literacy also means knowing when to log off for a screen-free break.
