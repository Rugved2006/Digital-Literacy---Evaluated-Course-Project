### 2.1 Definition of Digital Literacy

Digital literacy is the ability to find, evaluate, and communicate information through various digital platforms. It is not merely the technical skill of using a computer, but the cognitive ability to assess the credibility and impact of digital content.

3\. Social Media: Navigating the Digital Landscape
--------------------------------------------------

In this section, we analyze the dual nature of social media—as a tool for connection and a potential source of misinformation.

### 3.1 Best Practices (The Dos)

1.  **Fact-Checking:** Implementing "Lateral Reading" by verifying claims across multiple tabs and credible news outlets.
    
2.  **Privacy Governance:** Routine audits of platform-specific privacy settings to limit data harvesting.
    
3.  **Digital Footprint Management:** Understanding that content is permanent; practicing the "Front Page Test" (Would I want this on the front page of a newspaper?).
    

### 3.2 Risks to Avoid (The Don'ts)

1.  **Phishing and Malware:** Avoiding unsolicited links that compromise personal security.
    
2.  **Echo Chambers:** Recognizing how algorithms create filter bubbles that prevent exposure to diverse viewpoints.
    
3.  **Digital Fatigue:** Managing screen time to prevent negative mental health outcomes.
    

4\. Cyber Security and Safety
-----------------------------

Security is the foundation of digital literacy. This project emphasizes:

*   **Authentication:** Using Password+MFA strategies to secure accounts.
